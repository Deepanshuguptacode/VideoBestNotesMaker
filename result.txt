  <h3>ğŸ“Œ 2. Calculating the Median ğŸ“Œ</h3>

  <p>ğŸ’¡ <strong>Definition:</strong> The middle value in a sorted set of data. If there's an even number of elements, it's the average of the two middle values. ğŸ’¡</p>

  <p>ğŸ¯ <strong>Real-Life Example:</strong> Finding the "middle" salary in a company. ğŸ¯</p>

    <li><code>len(sorted_data[0]) // 2</code>: Finds the middle index of the sorted row (using integer division <code>//</code> to handle odd lengths).</li>
    <li><code>sorted_data[:, ... ]</code> then retrieves the median.</li>

    <li><code>axis=1</code>: Concatenates along the columns (joins horizontally). The tensors must have the same number of rows.</li>
  </ul>

  <h3>ğŸ“Œ 4. Finding Maximum and Minimum Values and Indices ğŸ“Œ</h3>

  <p>ğŸ’¡ <strong>Definition:</strong> Identifying the largest and smallest values in a tensor, and finding their positions (indices). ğŸ’¡</p>
